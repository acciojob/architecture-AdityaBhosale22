A Journey Through Time and Stone

Architecture is more than just construction; it is the art and science of designing and erecting buildings and other physical structures. It reflects culture, history, and technological advancement. From the pyramids of Giza to the modern skyscrapers of Dubai, these structures define the human experience and shape our environment.

Iconic Architectural Styles

Notre-Dame

Gothic Architecture (Mid-12th Century)

Characterized by pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses, this style allowed for taller, brighter structures. The emphasis was on verticality and light, perfectly exemplified by Notre-Dame de Paris and the Cologne Cathedral. It was a spiritual and technical revolution.

Bauhaus

Modernism (Early 20th Century)

Modern architecture rejected historical styles in favor of functional, geometric forms. The core principles were "form follows function" and the belief that materials like steel, concrete, and glass should be honestly expressed. Key movements include Bauhaus and International Style.
